# Landing / marketing page — brief

**Product:** ValidatedScale — runs validated psychometric instruments as rigorous studies. Protocol (declare instrument, identity mode, consent, scoring — locked at launch) → Field (live collection: open links, email invitations, coverage against a k-anonymity threshold) → Evidence (versioned scores, wave comparison, analysis-ready CSV/SPSS-friendly exports with codebooks).

**Audiences, in priority order:**
1. Academic researchers (occupational health, ergonomics, psychology) — care about: anonymous longitudinal linking via self-generated participant codes, IRB-friendly consent records, exports their statistician won't have to fix, EU hosting with no DPA hassle.
2. OSH consultants (EU) — care about: white-label per-client workspaces, legally mandated psychosocial risk assessments, compliance-ready reports.
3. Hospital / HR wellbeing officers — care about: continuous monitoring, k-protected dashboards nobody can re-identify individuals from.

**Voice:** measured, precise, quietly confident — a scientific instrument, not a SaaS hype page. Plain verbs, concrete claims. The current hero is "Measurement you can defend." — that register.

**Hard claim boundaries (legal — never violate):**
- Never name specific instruments (no MBI, COPSOQ, NMQ, PSS-10, OLBI…) as included, supported, or built-in. The platform runs *tenant-provided validated instruments* — instruments the customer has the right to use.
- Never show real questionnaire item text from any published instrument; invented generic items only.
- Never claim official validation, norms, or endorsement by instrument publishers.
- Safe claims: "validated-instrument-first", "your instruments, run rigorously", anonymous longitudinal linking, k-anonymity-enforced reporting, GDPR-native, EU-hosted, consent records, versioned scoring, codebook exports.

**Visual direction:** porcelain-light, generous whitespace, the calibrated-rail motif as the signature hero device (ticks, wave pips, an engraved threshold line). Serif (`.doc-title`) for the headline thesis, `.eyebrow` kickers, `.datum` mono for any number. One violet accent; graphite `.btn-ink` / violet `.btn-stain` CTAs. A dark `--color-console*` band is allowed as *one* section (e.g. showing the live Field console) — never the whole page. No stock-photo people; the product's own surfaces (protocol document, coverage meters, evidence tables) are the imagery, mocked with synthetic data.

**Sections worth exploring:** hero with live-reading rail; the three-phase story (Protocol / Field / Evidence); trust block (EU · GDPR · k-anonymity · consent, stated plainly); audience-specific value rows; a respondent-experience glimpse (mobile consent sheet); pricing placeholder; academic-credibility CTA ("write us into your next grant").
